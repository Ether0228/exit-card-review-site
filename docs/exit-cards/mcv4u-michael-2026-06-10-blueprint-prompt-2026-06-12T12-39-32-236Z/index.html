补救模块

E1

Confuses sign of f' with direction of f.

Mini lesson: f'(x) > 0 means the slope is positive, so f is increasing. f'(x) < 0 means slope negative, f decreasing.

E2

Thinks any critical point is an extremum.

Mini lesson: A critical point is a local extremum only if f' changes sign. If f' has same sign on both sides, it's not an extremum.

E3

Reverses concavity sign rule.

Mini lesson: f''(x) > 0 means f is concave up (like a cup). f''(x) < 0 means concave down (like a frown).

E4

Confuses inflection points with f'=0 or extrema.

Mini lesson: An inflection point occurs where f'' changes sign (concavity changes). It may or may not have f'=0.

E5

Cannot relate features of f, f', f'' graphs.

Mini lesson: Where f has a local max/min, f' crosses x-axis. Where f has inflection, f'' crosses x-axis. f' above x-axis means f increasing.

E6

Forgets oblique asymptotes when degree numerator = degree denominator + 1.

Mini lesson: When degree of numerator is exactly one more than denominator, there is an oblique (slant) asymptote found by polynomial long division.

E7

Does not verify sketch against derivative sign charts.

Mini lesson: Always check: on intervals where f'>0, graph must go up; where f'<0, go down. Concavity must match f'' sign.

E8

Cannot translate derivative conditions into graph shape.

Mini lesson: Break it down: f'>0 means increasing; f'<0 decreasing. f''>0 concave up; f''<0 concave down. Combine to sketch mentally.

E9

Thinks f''=0 alone gives inflection point.

Mini lesson: An inflection point requires f'' to change sign. f''=0 is necessary but not sufficient.

E10

Omits key features like asymptotes or inflection when describing a graph.

Mini lesson: When describing a graph from derivative info, list: domain, asymptotes, intercepts, intervals of increase/decrease, local extrema, concavity, inflection points.
